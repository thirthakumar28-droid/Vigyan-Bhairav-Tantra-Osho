Love is the easiest natural doorway out of the busy mind, because in a real moment of love the past and the future both disappear. How to Practice

Technique 48 of 112
Category: Tantric Union · Tags: sex · fire · beginning · awareness · energy
At the start of sexual union, keep attentive on the fire in the beginning, and so continuing, avoid the embers in the end.

In Plain English

During lovemaking, bring total awareness to the BEGINNING — the initial arousal, the first touch, the fire igniting. Stay at the beginning. Don’t rush toward climax (the “embers”). By keeping attention at the fire’s start, sexual energy rises upward rather than discharging downward. The act becomes meditation.

How to Practice

  1. Set the intention beforehand: Before lovemaking begins, both partners (or yourself, if practising with self-generated arousal) should agree: this is meditation, not performance. There is no goal, no climax to reach, no timeline. The BEGINNING is the destination. Discuss this openly — shared intention is crucial.
  2. Slow everything down: Begin physical contact extremely slowly. The first touch, the first kiss, the first caress — let each last ten times longer than usual. Bring total attention to the SENSATION of beginning. Feel the fire igniting — the first warmth, the first stirring of energy. This is where the technique lives.
  3. Keep attention on the fire, not the goal: As arousal builds, the mind will want to rush forward toward climax. This is the habit pattern. Instead, bring attention BACK to the beginning quality — the freshness, the first spark, the igniting. Stay at the point of ignition. Feel the energy at its SOURCE, not its destination.
  4. Notice where energy moves: Sexual energy naturally moves downward toward discharge. With awareness at the beginning (the source), the energy begins to move UPWARD — through the belly, the heart, the throat. You may feel warmth rising through the spine. Don’t force this — simply keep awareness at the fire’s start and notice where energy naturally wants to flow.
  5. Avoid the embers: “Embers” means the end — climax, discharge, conclusion. The sutra says: avoid this. Not through suppression or tension, but through ATTENTION. When attention remains at the beginning, the end simply doesn’t arrive. The fire keeps burning without becoming embers. Energy circulates rather than discharges.
  6. Ride the plateau: With practice, you discover a “plateau” state — highly aroused, deeply present, energy circulating upward, but without moving toward climax. This plateau can be sustained for extended periods (30 minutes, an hour, longer). The longer you stay, the more the energy transforms — from sexual heat to spiritual luminosity.
  7. Let the body be totally relaxed: Tension drives energy toward discharge. Relaxation allows energy to rise. Keep the body soft, especially the pelvis, belly, and jaw. Breathe deeply and slowly. A relaxed body is the vessel through which energy rises; a tense body is a closed system that can only discharge.
  8. The transformation: When sexual energy rises upward through awareness rather than discharging downward through tension, something remarkable happens: the energy reaches the heart (you feel overwhelming love), the throat (you may want to sing or cry), and the crown (states of meditation, bliss, ego-dissolution). The same energy that creates children can create consciousness.
  9. After practice: Lie together in stillness for at least 15 minutes. Don’t talk immediately. Let the energy settle. You may feel vibrating, expanded, deeply peaceful. This afterglow is the residue of transformed energy — savour it. It continues working even after the practice ends.
  10. Solo practice: This technique can be practised alone. During self-generated arousal, apply the same principle: stay at the beginning, keep attention on the fire of initial arousal, avoid rushing toward conclusion. The energy rises the same way. A partner is helpful but not essential.

Category: Tantric Union · Tags: embrace · shaking · energy · surrender
When in such embrace your senses are shaken as leaves, enter this shaking.

In Plain English

During embrace (sexual or deep holding), the body may tremble or shake with energy. Don’t suppress the shaking — ENTER it. Let the body vibrate freely. The shaking is energy releasing old patterns. Enter it totally and it becomes a doorway to ecstatic dissolution.

How to Practice

  1. Understand the shaking: When two bodies meet in deep embrace — especially with sexual energy present — a trembling often arises. Most people suppress it (embarrassment, control, fear). This sutra says: do the opposite. The shaking is not weakness or nervousness — it is ENERGY moving through the body, clearing pathways, dissolving blocks.
  2. Create the conditions: Hold your partner (or be held) in a deep, full-body embrace. Not a brief hug but a sustained holding — chest to chest, belly to belly, as much skin contact as possible. Breathe together. Let the embrace last many minutes without agenda. Eventually, energy will build between the bodies.
  3. Wait for the trembling: As energy builds, a subtle vibration begins. It may start in the legs, the arms, the belly, or the spine. It may be barely perceptible at first — a fine tremor, a quivering. Don’t look for it with the mind; FEEL for it with the body. When it comes, it comes on its own.
  4. Don’t suppress it: The habitual response is to tighten muscles and stop the trembling. Every social conditioning says: stay in control, don’t shake, don’t lose composure. Drop all of this. Allow the trembling completely. Give it permission. Let the body do whatever it wants to do.
  5. ENTER the shaking: This is the key instruction. Not just “allow” but ENTER. Become the shaking. Don’t stand apart and watch yourself shake — dissolve INTO the vibration. Let your identity merge with the trembling. You are not someone who is shaking; you ARE the shaking itself. There is no “you” separate from the vibration.
  6. Amplify naturally: Once you enter the shaking without resistance, it often amplifies on its own. The trembling may become stronger, spread through the whole body, become rhythmic or chaotic. Let it. The body is releasing stored tension, emotion, energy blocks. It knows what to do. Your job is only to not interfere.
  7. The senses dissolve: The sutra says “your senses are shaken as leaves.” When the trembling is total, the usual sensory boundaries dissolve. You no longer know where your body ends and your partner’s begins. Sight, hearing, touch all merge into one vibrating field of energy. The separate self shakes apart like leaves in a storm.
  8. Ecstatic dissolution: When the shaking is total and the senses have merged, the ego dissolves. There is no longer “you” and “partner” — there is only vibrating energy, ecstatic and boundless. This is the tantric dissolution — two becoming one, and the one dissolving into the whole. Stay here as long as it lasts.
  9. Solo practice: You can practise this alone. Stand with knees slightly bent and allow the body to shake. Start mechanically if needed — shake the hands, arms, legs — then let the shaking take over. Enter it completely. Osho’s Kundalini Meditation uses this principle: 15 minutes of shaking followed by 15 minutes of stillness.
  10. After the shaking: When the shaking subsides naturally, lie or sit in complete stillness. Don’t move for 10–15 minutes. The body will feel deeply relaxed, cleared, vibrating with subtle aliveness. The mind will be profoundly silent. This post-shaking stillness is where the deepest meditation happens — the body has cleared the way.

In Plain English

You don’t need a partner. Simply REMEMBER a moment of deep union — the energy, the feeling, the dissolution. Memory can recreate the same energetic state. Feel the fire of union arising from memory alone. This transforms sexual energy into spiritual energy without physical contact.

How to Practice

  1. Choose your memory: Recall a moment of deep union — sexual, romantic, or even a moment of profound connection (holding a newborn, merging with nature, deep friendship).   2. Create the conditions: Sit or lie down in a quiet, private space. Close your eyes. Breathe slowly and deeply. Let the body relax completely. Give yourself at least 20 minutes. This technique works best in the evening or early morning when the mind is naturally more receptive.
  3. Don’t just think — FEEL the memory: The mind’s tendency is to remember visually or narratively (“we were in that place, we did that thing”). Drop the story. It may feel like meditation, like love without object, like spacious awareness, like inner light. Don’t analyse it — let it be. This is the “transformation” the sutra promises: sexual energy has become spiritual awareness.

How to Practice

  1. Lie in bed, ready for sleep: This is an in-bed, eyes-closed, falling-asleep technique. All preparation is done. Lights off. Body in corpse pose or your natural sleep position. This is the last thing you do before sleep takes you.
  2. Feel the body as elements: Begin by recognising what the body is made of. The EARTH element: bones, muscles, flesh — everything solid. The WATER element: blood, saliva, lymph — everything fluid.   6. Air dissolving: The breath becomes thinner, lighter, barely perceptible. The air in the lungs returns to the atmosphere. Breathing becomes so subtle it almost stops. The air element leaves — you are no longer breathing; the cosmos breathes through what remains of you.
  7. Space dissolving: Finally, even the space within the body dissolves into the infinite space of the cosmos. The boundary between inner space and outer space disappears. There is no longer a “body” with space inside it — there is just space. Infinite, unbounded, cosmic space. And awareness floating in it.
  8. What remains: When all five elements have been consumed — when the body has been completely returned to the cosmos — what remains? Only consciousness. Pure awareness without a body. This is what persists through sleep. This is what persists through death. This is what you ARE. Let this bodiless awareness enter sleep. Let it rest in the cosmic space it has always been part of.
  9. Fall asleep in dissolution: Most people will fall asleep during this process — often during the earth or water phase. This is perfect. The dissolution continues unconsciously, and the quality of sleep is profoundly different — deeper, more restful, more cosmic. You wake feeling like you dissolved and were reassembled fresh.
  10. Morning reassembly: Upon waking, notice: the elements reassemble. Space condenses into body-space. Air fills the lungs with a deep breath. Fire returns as warmth floods the body. Water returns as you feel fluids moving. Earth returns as solidity and weight. You are reconstructed from the cosmos each morning. Every day is a rebirth.

The Techniques for Tantric Union

Four techniques that use desire, sexual energy, embrace, and reunion as doorways to transcendence. Tantra does not reject desire — it transforms desire into a vehicle. The fire that usually burns downward is turned upward into awakening.

4 techniques · ~8 min read · works fully offline

About these techniques

These four techniques are drawn from the Vigyan Bhairav Tantra, a Sanskrit text dated to roughly 5,000 years ago, in which Shiva tells his consort Devi 112 ways to recognise the divine within ordinary life.

Osho (1931–1990), an Indian mystic, gave a series of talks unpacking each one; that material was published as The Book of Secrets. The tantra sutras form the eleventh major category — using sexual energy, embrace, memory of union, and the joy of reunion as vehicles for transformation.

These techniques are revolutionary because they do not ask you to suppress desire. Instead, they ask you to bring TOTAL AWARENESS to desire. When awareness meets desire, desire becomes a ladder rather than a trap. The same energy that creates biological life can create spiritual rebirth.

How to use this page

Pick ONE technique. Practise it for 21 days. Don’t shop, don’t switch, don’t combine. Twenty-one days is the threshold the book itself recommends — long enough for resistance to surface, peak, and pass.

If on day 7 you think it isn’t working — that is the work. Stay.

Key principle: These tantra techniques use desire AS the path, not against it. Total awareness brought to desire transforms it. You are not suppressing anything — you are illuminating everything.

New to tantric meditation?

Start with Technique 4 — joyously seeing a long-absent friend or Technique 3 — remembering union. Both can be practised alone and require no partner. They build the capacity for awareness-in-feeling that the deeper techniques require.

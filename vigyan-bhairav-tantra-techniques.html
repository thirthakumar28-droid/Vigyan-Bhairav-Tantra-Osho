The Techniques for Tantric Union

Four techniques that use desire, sexual energy, embrace, and reunion as doorways to transcendence. Tantra does not reject desire — it transforms desire into a vehicle. The fire that usually burns downward is turned upward into awakening.

4 techniques · ~8 min read · works fully offline

About these techniques

These four techniques are drawn from the Vigyan Bhairav Tantra, a Sanskrit text dated to roughly 5,000 years ago, in which Shiva tells his consort Devi 112 ways to recognise the divine within ordinary life.

Osho (1931–1990), an Indian mystic, gave a series of talks unpacking each one; that material was published as The Book of Secrets. The tantra sutras form the eleventh major category — using sexual energy, embrace, memory of union, and the joy of reunion as vehicles for transformation.

These techniques are revolutionary because they do not ask you to suppress desire. Instead, they ask you to bring TOTAL AWARENESS to desire. When awareness meets desire, desire becomes a ladder rather than a trap. The same energy that creates biological life can create spiritual rebirth.

How to use this page

Pick ONE technique. Practise it for 21 days. Don’t shop, don’t switch, don’t combine. Twenty-one days is the threshold the book itself recommends — long enough for resistance to surface, peak, and pass.

If on day 7 you think it isn’t working — that is the work. Stay.

Key principle: These tantra techniques use desire AS the path, not against it. Total awareness brought to desire transforms it. You are not suppressing anything — you are illuminating everything.

New to tantric meditation?

Start with Technique 4 — joyously seeing a long-absent friend or Technique 3 — remembering union. Both can be practised alone and require no partner. They build the capacity for awareness-in-feeling that the deeper techniques require.

Technique 48 of 112
Category: Tantric Union · Tags: sex · fire · beginning · awareness · energy
At the start of sexual union, keep attentive on the fire in the beginning, and so continuing, avoid the embers in the end.

In Plain English

During lovemaking, bring total awareness to the BEGINNING — the initial arousal, the first touch, the fire igniting. Stay at the beginning. Don’t rush toward climax (the “embers”). By keeping attention at the fire’s start, sexual energy rises upward rather than discharging downward. The act becomes meditation.

How to Practice

  1. Set the intention beforehand: Before lovemaking begins, both partners (or yourself, if practising with self-generated arousal) should agree: this is meditation, not performance. There is no goal, no climax to reach, no timeline. The BEGINNING is the destination. Discuss this openly — shared intention is crucial.
  2. Slow everything down: Begin physical contact extremely slowly. The first touch, the first kiss, the first caress — let each last ten times longer than usual. Bring total attention to the SENSATION of beginning. Feel the fire igniting — the first warmth, the first stirring of energy. This is where the technique lives.
  3. Keep attention on the fire, not the goal: As arousal builds, the mind will want to rush forward toward climax. This is the habit pattern. Instead, bring attention BACK to the beginning quality — the freshness, the first spark, the igniting. Stay at the point of ignition. Feel the energy at its SOURCE, not its destination.
  4. Notice where energy moves: Sexual energy naturally moves downward toward discharge. With awareness at the beginning (the source), the energy begins to move UPWARD — through the belly, the heart, the throat. You may feel warmth rising through the spine. Don’t force this — simply keep awareness at the fire’s start and notice where energy naturally wants to flow.
  5. Avoid the embers: “Embers” means the end — climax, discharge, conclusion. The sutra says: avoid this. Not through suppression or tension, but through ATTENTION. When attention remains at the beginning, the end simply doesn’t arrive. The fire keeps burning without becoming embers. Energy circulates rather than discharges.
  6. Ride the plateau: With practice, you discover a “plateau” state — highly aroused, deeply present, energy circulating upward, but without moving toward climax. This plateau can be sustained for extended periods (30 minutes, an hour, longer). The longer you stay, the more the energy transforms — from sexual heat to spiritual luminosity.
  7. Let the body be totally relaxed: Tension drives energy toward discharge. Relaxation allows energy to rise. Keep the body soft, especially the pelvis, belly, and jaw. Breathe deeply and slowly. A relaxed body is the vessel through which energy rises; a tense body is a closed system that can only discharge.
  8. The transformation: When sexual energy rises upward through awareness rather than discharging downward through tension, something remarkable happens: the energy reaches the heart (you feel overwhelming love), the throat (you may want to sing or cry), and the crown (states of meditation, bliss, ego-dissolution). The same energy that creates children can create consciousness.
  9. After practice: Lie together in stillness for at least 15 minutes. Don’t talk immediately. Let the energy settle. You may feel vibrating, expanded, deeply peaceful. This afterglow is the residue of transformed energy — savour it. It continues working even after the practice ends.
  10. Solo practice: This technique can be practised alone. During self-generated arousal, apply the same principle: stay at the beginning, keep attention on the fire of initial arousal, avoid rushing toward conclusion. The energy rises the same way. A partner is helpful but not essential.

  • Guilt or shame about using sexuality as meditation — tantra views sexual energy as sacred; release religious conditioning
  • Forcing the energy upward rather than allowing it — relaxation and awareness guide energy naturally; force creates blockage

Questions From The Book

Why does Shiva use “fire” and “embers” as metaphors?

Chapter 41, "The Fire in the Beginning"

Osho explains: fire is alive, dynamic, rising upward. Embers are dead, spent, sinking downward. The beginning of sexual energy is like fire — alive, upward-moving, transformative. The climax is like embers — the fire has spent itself, the energy collapses. By staying with the fire (the living, rising energy) and avoiding the embers (the spent, collapsed energy), you ride the upward flame indefinitely. The metaphor teaches the direction: UP with the fire, not DOWN with the embers.

Is this technique about suppressing orgasm?

Chapter 41

Osho is emphatic: NO. Suppression creates tension and neurosis. This technique is about TRANSCENDING orgasm through awareness, not suppressing it through will. When awareness is total at the beginning, the energy naturally transforms rather than discharges. It is not that you STOP the orgasm; rather, the orgasm is replaced by something higher — a full-body, continuous state of bliss that makes genital orgasm seem small by comparison. You don’t lose anything; you gain everything.

Modern Commentary

AI-distilled, not from Osho

What this is really pointing at: Neurologically, orgasm is a discharge event — a rapid release of accumulated tension. But the arousal phase activates the parasympathetic nervous system, producing states of deep relaxation and expanded awareness. By extending the arousal phase indefinitely (staying in “the fire”) while avoiding the sympathetic discharge event (“the embers”), you maintain a state of expanded consciousness sustained by sexual energy.

Research parallel: Studies on non-orgasmic sexual meditation practices (karezza, Daoist sexual cultivation) show that prolonged arousal without discharge correlates with elevated oxytocin, sustained dopamine (rather than the post-orgasmic dopamine crash), and subjective reports of expanded awareness, deep bonding, and spiritual experiences.

One-line essenceStay with the fire of beginning. Don’t rush to the embers of ending. When awareness illuminates desire at its source, the energy rises — and sex becomes samadhi.
Technique 49 of 112
Category: Tantric Union · Tags: embrace · shaking · energy · surrender
When in such embrace your senses are shaken as leaves, enter this shaking.

In Plain English

During embrace (sexual or deep holding), the body may tremble or shake with energy. Don’t suppress the shaking — ENTER it. Let the body vibrate freely. The shaking is energy releasing old patterns. Enter it totally and it becomes a doorway to ecstatic dissolution.

How to Practice

  1. Understand the shaking: When two bodies meet in deep embrace — especially with sexual energy present — a trembling often arises. Most people suppress it (embarrassment, control, fear). This sutra says: do the opposite. The shaking is not weakness or nervousness — it is ENERGY moving through the body, clearing pathways, dissolving blocks.
  2. Create the conditions: Hold your partner (or be held) in a deep, full-body embrace. Not a brief hug but a sustained holding — chest to chest, belly to belly, as much skin contact as possible. Breathe together. Let the embrace last many minutes without agenda. Eventually, energy will build between the bodies.
  3. Wait for the trembling: As energy builds, a subtle vibration begins. It may start in the legs, the arms, the belly, or the spine. It may be barely perceptible at first — a fine tremor, a quivering. Don’t look for it with the mind; FEEL for it with the body. When it comes, it comes on its own.
  4. Don’t suppress it: The habitual response is to tighten muscles and stop the trembling. Every social conditioning says: stay in control, don’t shake, don’t lose composure. Drop all of this. Allow the trembling completely. Give it permission. Let the body do whatever it wants to do.
  5. ENTER the shaking: This is the key instruction. Not just “allow” but ENTER. Become the shaking. Don’t stand apart and watch yourself shake — dissolve INTO the vibration. Let your identity merge with the trembling. You are not someone who is shaking; you ARE the shaking itself. There is no “you” separate from the vibration.
  6. Amplify naturally: Once you enter the shaking without resistance, it often amplifies on its own. The trembling may become stronger, spread through the whole body, become rhythmic or chaotic. Let it. The body is releasing stored tension, emotion, energy blocks. It knows what to do. Your job is only to not interfere.
  7. The senses dissolve: The sutra says “your senses are shaken as leaves.” When the trembling is total, the usual sensory boundaries dissolve. You no longer know where your body ends and your partner’s begins. Sight, hearing, touch all merge into one vibrating field of energy. The separate self shakes apart like leaves in a storm.
  8. Ecstatic dissolution: When the shaking is total and the senses have merged, the ego dissolves. There is no longer “you” and “partner” — there is only vibrating energy, ecstatic and boundless. This is the tantric dissolution — two becoming one, and the one dissolving into the whole. Stay here as long as it lasts.
  9. Solo practice: You can practise this alone. Stand with knees slightly bent and allow the body to shake. Start mechanically if needed — shake the hands, arms, legs — then let the shaking take over. Enter it completely. Osho’s Kundalini Meditation uses this principle: 15 minutes of shaking followed by 15 minutes of stillness.
  10. After the shaking: When the shaking subsides naturally, lie or sit in complete stillness. Don’t move for 10–15 minutes. The body will feel deeply relaxed, cleared, vibrating with subtle aliveness. The mind will be profoundly silent. This post-shaking stillness is where the deepest meditation happens — the body has cleared the way.

Questions From The Book

What is this energy that causes the shaking?

Chapter 42, "Enter the Shaking"

Osho explains: it is kundalini — the coiled energy at the base of the spine that, when awakened through deep contact or arousal, begins to move upward. As it moves, it encounters blocks — muscular tensions, emotional armoring, energy stagnation. The shaking IS the energy clearing these blocks. Each tremor is a block dissolving. When all blocks are cleared, the energy flows freely and the shaking transforms into stillness — the stillness of total flow, like a river moving so smoothly it appears still.

Why does the sutra use the image of leaves?

Chapter 42

Osho notes: leaves shake because they have no resistance. The wind blows and the leaf responds totally — it doesn’t try to stay still, doesn’t fight the wind, doesn’t hold itself rigid. It simply moves with whatever force moves through it. This is the quality the sutra asks of you: be like a leaf. When energy-wind blows through your body, don’t resist, don’t control — respond as totally and as innocently as a leaf in the breeze.

Modern Commentary

AI-distilled, not from Osho

What this is really pointing at: Neurogenic tremoring (TRE — Tension and Trauma Release Exercises) is a modern therapeutic approach that deliberately activates the body’s natural tremor mechanism to release stored stress and trauma. The principle is identical: the body knows how to shake off held tension if we let it. Peter Levine’s Somatic Experiencing similarly works with the body’s trembling response as a healing mechanism.

Polyvagal perspective: The shaking represents the body completing arrested fight/flight responses. When the nervous system is held in a state of safety (the embrace), stored survival energy can discharge through tremoring. The social engagement system (activated by the embrace) creates the safety needed for the body to release what it has held.

One-line essenceWhen energy shakes you like a leaf in the wind, don’t resist — enter the shaking completely. Let the trembling dissolve the boundary between you and existence.
Technique 50 of 112
Category: Tantric Union · Tags: memory · sexual-energy · transformation · alone
Even remembering union, without the embrace, transformation.

In Plain English

You don’t need a partner. Simply REMEMBER a moment of deep union — the energy, the feeling, the dissolution. Memory can recreate the same energetic state. Feel the fire of union arising from memory alone. This transforms sexual energy into spiritual energy without physical contact.

How to Practice

  1. Choose your memory: Recall a moment of deep union — sexual, romantic, or even a moment of profound connection (holding a newborn, merging with nature, deep friendship). The key quality you are looking for is: a moment when the boundary between you and another dissolved. When two became one, however briefly.
  2. Create the conditions: Sit or lie down in a quiet, private space. Close your eyes. Breathe slowly and deeply. Let the body relax completely. Give yourself at least 20 minutes. This technique works best in the evening or early morning when the mind is naturally more receptive.
  3. Don’t just think — FEEL the memory: The mind’s tendency is to remember visually or narratively (“we were in that place, we did that thing”). Drop the story. Instead, feel the ENERGY of the moment. What did your body feel like? Where was the warmth? Where was the openness? What was the quality of consciousness in that moment of union?
  4. Rebuild the feeling in the body: Let the memory recreate its physical sensations. If there was warmth in the chest, feel it NOW. If there was tingling in the spine, feel it NOW. If there was a sense of boundarylessness, feel it NOW. The body does not distinguish between present experience and vividly felt memory. The same neural circuits fire.
  5. Let the energy build: As you dwell in the felt memory, notice: energy begins to stir. The same sexual/life energy that was present during the actual union begins to awaken through memory alone. This is the discovery: the energy is YOURS, not dependent on another person. The other person was a catalyst, but the energy belongs to you.
  6. Stay with the fire, not the embers: As with Technique 1, keep attention on the BEGINNING quality of the energy — the igniting, the freshness, the rising flame. Don’t let the memory move toward its conclusion. Stay at the peak of connection — the moment of maximum dissolution — and let the energy circulate without discharge.
  7. Feel the transformation happening: With sustained attention on the energetic memory of union, something shifts. The energy that began as sexual (located in the pelvis) begins to rise — through belly, heart, throat, crown. It changes quality as it rises: from hot desire to warm love to cool bliss to vast spaciousness. This is sublimation — not by suppression but by awareness.
  8. Discover your own completeness: The deepest teaching of this technique: you don’t need another person to access the state of union. The union was always happening WITHIN you — the other person simply gave you permission to feel it. Once you can access it through memory alone, you know: this energy, this dissolution, this bliss is your own nature.
  9. Rest in the transformed state: When the energy has risen and transformed, rest in whatever state has opened. It may feel like meditation, like love without object, like spacious awareness, like inner light. Don’t analyse it — let it be. This is the “transformation” the sutra promises: sexual energy has become spiritual awareness.
  10. Practice regularly: Do this daily for 21 days. Each session deepens the capacity to access the energetic state of union independently. Over time, you may find you can access the state without even needing the memory — the energy is always available; you simply learn to turn toward it.

Questions From The Book

Why does Shiva say “even remembering” — what makes memory so powerful?

Chapter 42, "Memory as Alchemy"

Osho explains: the body cannot tell the difference between a vividly relived memory and present experience. The same hormones release, the same energy moves, the same neural pathways activate. This is why nightmares produce real sweat and sexual dreams produce real arousal. If you can remember deeply enough — not just mentally but with the whole body — the energy state of union recreates itself completely. Memory is not about the past; it is a present-moment activation of stored energy patterns.

Does this technique work for people who have never experienced sexual union?

Chapter 42

Osho says: yes, because “union” is not limited to sexual union. Any moment of deep connection — with nature, with music, with a moment of beauty, with a loved one (even non-sexually) — contains the same quality of dissolution. Remember ANY moment when the boundary of self dissolved even slightly. The energy is the same. Tantra uses sexuality as the most obvious example because it is the most common experience of ego-dissolution, but it is not the only one.

Modern Commentary

AI-distilled, not from Osho

What this is really pointing at: Neuroscience confirms that vividly recalled experiences activate largely the same brain regions as the original experience (the “reactivation principle”). fMRI studies show that remembering sexual arousal activates the same hypothalamic and reward circuits as actual arousal. This technique leverages neuroplasticity: by repeatedly activating the energy pattern of union through memory, you strengthen the neural pathway until it becomes accessible at will.

Therapeutic application: This principle is used in somatic therapy — recalling a moment of safety and connection to activate the corresponding physiological state. The body is a storehouse of experience-patterns that can be re-activated through intentional remembering. This is not mere imagination; it is re-embodiment of a lived state.

One-line essenceThe fire of union lives in your memory. Recall it — not as thought but as energy — and discover that transformation needs no partner. The alchemy is always available within.
Technique 51 of 112
Category: Tantric Union · Tags: joy · reunion · permeation · love
On joyously seeing a long-absent friend, permeate this joy.

In Plain English

When meeting a beloved friend after long absence, CATCH the joy. Don’t let it pass unconsciously. Feel it fully — let it permeate every cell. That joy of reunion is the same quality as divine love. Permeate it, become it, and you taste the eternal.

How to Practice

  1. Understand “permeate”: Normally when joy arises — at seeing a loved one, receiving good news, experiencing beauty — we feel it briefly and then it passes. We are too distracted, too much in our heads, to let it fully FILL us. “Permeate” means: let the joy soak into every cell, every corner of consciousness. Like a sponge dropped in water — saturate completely.
  2. Watch for the moment: The technique begins BEFORE the reunion. Whenever you know you will meet someone you love after long absence (arriving at an airport, opening a door, receiving a call), prepare your awareness. Tell yourself: “When the joy comes, I will CATCH it. I will not let it pass unconsciously.”
  3. The first instant: When you see the friend — in that FIRST instant of recognition, that flash of joy — CATCH IT. Don’t let the mind immediately start talking, planning, asking questions. Stay with the pure flash of joy for even 3–5 seconds before engaging. That first flash is the purest form of the energy.
  4. Let it permeate: Instead of the joy staying in the head (as an idea: “I’m happy to see you”), let it sink downward into the body. Feel joy in the chest, the belly, the legs, the arms. Let it fill the WHOLE body. Not just “I feel happy” but the entire organism vibrating with joy. Head to toes. Every cell celebrating.
  5. Become the joy: Take it further: not “I FEEL joy” but “I AM joy.” There is no separate “you” who experiences joy — there is only joy, permeating everything. The boundary between feeler and feeling dissolves. You don’t HAVE joy; you ARE joy. This is permeation at its deepest.
  6. Don’t rush into activity: The social habit is to immediately embrace, talk, catch up, make plans. These are all fine LATER. But first, take 30 seconds to simply BE with the joy. Let it permeate. Then engage. Those 30 seconds of pure permeation teach you more about the nature of consciousness than years of philosophy.
  7. Extend to smaller joys: Once you practise with the intense joy of reunion, apply the same technique to smaller joys: seeing a flower bloom, tasting good food, hearing a favourite song, watching a sunset. CATCH the joy. Let it permeate. Become it. Every joy — however small — is a doorway to the divine when fully permeated.
  8. The teaching: What this technique reveals is that joy does not come FROM the friend, the flower, or the sunset. Joy is your natural state — these external triggers simply give you PERMISSION to feel what is always there. By practising permeation, you learn to access joy directly, without needing a trigger. The friend was the excuse; the joy is your nature.
  9. Practice without trigger: After weeks of permeating joy whenever it naturally arises, try this: sit quietly and REMEMBER the joy of reunion (similar to Technique 3). Let the memory permeate you. Then let go of even the memory — can you feel joy with NO cause? This is the ultimate goal: causeless joy, your natural state rediscovered.
  10. Daily awareness: Throughout the day, notice every moment of joy — however fleeting — and practise permeation. The barista smiles at you: permeate. A child laughs nearby: permeate. You complete a task: permeate. Each micro-permeation teaches the body that joy is always available. Over weeks, the background state shifts from neutral to joyful.

Questions From The Book

Why does Shiva place this technique among the tantric sutras?

Chapter 42, "Permeate This Joy"

Osho explains: because tantra is the path of using positive emotions as vehicles for transcendence. Just as sexual energy can become a vehicle (Techniques 1-3), so can JOY. Joy is love-energy in its purest, most accessible form. When joy is fully permeated, the same dissolution happens as in sexual union — the separate self dissolves into the feeling. The difference is only intensity: sex is the strongest trigger, but joy is the most available. This technique brings tantra into ordinary daily life.

What is the difference between “feeling joy” and “permeating joy”?

Chapter 42

Osho distinguishes: “feeling joy” maintains the duality — there is a “you” who feels and a “joy” that is felt. “Permeating joy” dissolves the duality — joy fills every cell until there is no space left for a separate “you.” Permeation means: there is nowhere in you where joy is NOT. The feeler becomes the feeling. The experiencer becomes the experience. THAT is the tantric method applied to joy — dissolution through total immersion.

Modern Commentary

AI-distilled, not from Osho

What this is really pointing at: Positive psychology research shows that “savouring” — deliberately attending to and prolonging positive experiences — significantly increases well-being and life satisfaction. But this technique goes beyond savouring: it asks you to dissolve INTO the positive experience until the experiencer disappears. It is the difference between tasting chocolate and BECOMING the taste.

Neuroplasticity of joy: Rick Hanson’s research (“Hardwiring Happiness”) shows that deliberately dwelling in positive experiences for 10+ seconds converts them from short-term to long-term neural structure changes. This sutra asks for much more than 10 seconds — it asks for TOTAL permeation — which presumably creates much deeper neural restructuring toward a joyful default state.

One-line essenceWhen joy arises, don’t just feel it — become it. Let it permeate every cell until there is no “you” left, only joy. That joy without a feeler is the taste of the divine.

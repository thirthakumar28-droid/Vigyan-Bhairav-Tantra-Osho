Critical Disclaimer — Read Before Practising

Practise at your own discretion and responsibility.

These are contemplative practices, not medical or psychological treatment. They are not a substitute for professional mental-health care, therapy, or medical advice. If you have any serious health condition, consult a qualified professional before beginning.

Some techniques carry specific risks. Read the safety section below carefully and respect the cautions listed in each file:

  • If you have severe mental-health conditions (psychosis, severe trauma, suicidal ideation, dissociative disorders), do not begin without consulting a qualified professional. Avoid identity-dissolution and void techniques.
  • If you have cardiovascular conditions, avoid breath retention, intense kundalini-rising practices, and prolonged loud chanting.
  • If you are pregnant, avoid intense breath techniques, deep pelvic-energy work, and forceful kundalini practices.
  • If you are recovering from recent surgery or acute injury, avoid pain-as-doorway and intense postural techniques.
  • If you have glaucoma or eye conditions, avoid pressure-on-eyeballs techniques.
  • If overwhelm arises during practice, stop immediately. Open your eyes. Ground through body and breath. Drink water. Walk in nature.

The 21-day commitment described in this collection is intended for committed seekers, not casual experimenters. Some techniques (especially Tantric Union and energy work) require maturity, discernment, and respect for yourself and any partner.

When in doubt, choose the gentler technique. When unsure, do less, not more.

Safety & Cautions Index

Specific cautions across the 15 categories. Read these before choosing a technique.

Cardiovascular conditions

  • Avoid Light Tech #1 (light rays up the spine with sustained energy buildup).
  • Avoid breath retention in any technique — modify by allowing soft breath to flow.
  • Avoid prolonged loud chanting (Sound Tech #5, intense AUM).
  • Avoid intense kundalini-rising practices — favour gentle gradual variants.

Pregnancy

  • Avoid intense breath techniques with retention or rapid breathing.
  • Avoid deep pelvic-floor energy work and forceful kundalini practices.
  • Favour gentle techniques: sky-gazing, witnessing, heart, sound (soft).

Recent surgery or acute injury

  • Avoid pain-as-doorway (Centering Tech #21) until acute injury is medically cleared.
  • Avoid intense postural techniques (boat pose / unsupported sitting) if joints/spine are healing.

Severe mental-health conditions

  • For psychosis, severe PTSD, dissociative disorders, or active suicidal ideation, consult a qualified professional before any practice.
  • Avoid: Void Tech #1 (deprivation), Death Tech #1 (fire dissolution), Imagination Tech #1 (formless), and any technique involving identity dissolution.
  • Favour grounding techniques: heart-space, palming, sensory awareness, fresh-seeing.

Glaucoma and eye conditions

  • Avoid closing the seven openings (Centering Tech #5) — involves pressure on eyeballs.
  • Modify palming (Sensory Tech #7) to feather-light contact only; do not press.

Vertigo and motion sickness

  • Avoid Centering Tech #2 (vehicle swaying) and any spinning practices.

History of dissociation

  • Build a foundation with grounding practices (sensory, breath, heart) before attempting void or dissolution work.
  • Always end sessions with body-grounding: feel the feet, the breath, the room.

When in doubt, choose the gentler technique.

About

The Vigyan Bhairav Tantra

An ancient Sanskrit text dated to roughly 5,000 years ago, presented as a dialogue between Shiva and his consort Devi. Devi asks: "How can I know the divine?" Shiva replies with 112 techniques — methods, devices, doorways — covering breath, sound, light, witnessing, centering, devotion, and more. Each technique is a path; one is enough.

Osho (1931–1990)

An Indian mystic and teacher who, between 1972 and 1973, gave 80 talks on this text in Mumbai. Those talks were transcribed and published as The Book of Secrets. His insistence: do not believe; experiment. The techniques are scientific in spirit — they either work for you or they do not. Try one for 21 days, observe, decide.

The Book of Secrets

Osho's commentary on each of the 112 techniques is the source for the distillations in this collection. Many techniques are accompanied by Osho's stories, contextual teaching, and questions from his audience — all of which informed the "Questions From The Book" sections in each file.

This Project

Fifteen self-contained HTML files — one per category — plus this index. Each file is a digital companion to the original book: the sutra, plain-English explanation, step-by-step practice, signs of progress, common pitfalls, and modern commentary. Each works fully offline. There are no servers, no tracking, no analytics — only the techniques.

How to use this collection

Read this page first. Take the disclaimer and safety sections seriously. Use the situation matcher above, browse the category grid, or simply press the random button if you trust your intuition. When you find a technique that resonates, commit to 21 days. Don't shop. Don't switch. The discipline is the work.

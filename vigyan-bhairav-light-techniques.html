The Techniques for Light & Gazing

Ten techniques that use inner light, outer darkness, fire, sky, and the luminosity of awareness itself as doorways to the divine. Light is consciousness made visible; follow it inward and you become the source.

10 techniques · ~14 min read · works fully offline

About these techniques

These ten techniques are drawn from the Vigyan Bhairav Tantra, a Sanskrit text dated to roughly 5,000 years ago, in which Shiva tells his consort Devi 112 ways to recognise the divine within ordinary life.

Osho (1931–1990), an Indian mystic, gave a series of talks unpacking each one; that material was published as The Book of Secrets. The light and gazing sutras form the fourth major category — using inner luminosity, outer darkness, fire, sky-gazing, and the radiance of pure attention to dissolve the boundary between seer and seen.

These techniques span the full spectrum: from visualising light rising through the spine, to staring into a black night, to realising that awareness itself is luminous. Some are gentle beginner practices; others are among the most powerful and potentially overwhelming in the entire tantra.

How to use this page

Pick ONE technique. Practise it for 21 days. Don’t shop, don’t switch, don’t combine. Twenty-one days is the threshold the book itself recommends — long enough for resistance to surface, peak, and pass.

If on day 7 you think it isn’t working — that is the work. Stay.

Critical safety note: Techniques 1, 2, and 5 involve powerful energy work. Never leave these incomplete mid-session. Always bring the energy to the crown and release. Read all cautions before beginning.

New to light meditation?

Start with Technique 4 — the clear sky or Technique 9 — wherever attention alights. Both require nothing special and are safe for all levels. Spend 20–40 minutes a day for 21 days. Then return for what comes next.

Vigyan Bhairav Tantra
A ~5,000-year-old Sanskrit text presented as a dialogue between Shiva and Devi, listing 112 meditation techniques.
sahasrar
The thousand-petalled lotus at the crown of the head — the highest energy centre in the yogic system. When kundalini reaches here, the individual dissolves into the cosmic.
chakra
An energy centre in the subtle body. The seven main chakras run along the spine from base (muladhara) to crown (sahasrar).
kundalini
The dormant energy said to lie coiled at the base of the spine. When awakened, it rises through the chakras toward the crown.
sushumna
The central energy channel running along the spine through which kundalini rises. Flanked by ida (left/lunar) and pingala (right/solar).
prana
Life-force energy. Breath is its grossest form; light and attention are subtler forms. All techniques ultimately work with prana.
third eye
The ajna chakra, located between the eyebrows. Associated with insight, inner vision, and the dissolution of duality.
turiya
The fourth state — beyond waking, dreaming, and deep sleep. Pure awareness that witnesses all three states without being any of them.
Essenes
An ancient Jewish mystical sect (c. 200 BCE – 100 CE) that, according to Osho, experienced God as absolute darkness — the formless, the void before creation.
aura
The subtle luminous field said to surround living beings. In these techniques, imagining the body as an aura of light helps dissolve the sense of solidity.
prakriti
Nature or matter in Sankhya philosophy — the manifest world as distinguished from purusha (pure consciousness). The fire technique burns prakriti to reveal purusha.

Practise on your own discretion — adjust each technique to your own body and mind. Some techniques in this category can produce intense experiences. If you feel overwhelmed, open your eyes, ground through your feet, and breathe normally.

Technique 70 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: spine · chakras · light · energy · visualization
Consider your essence as light rays rising from centre to centre up the vertebrae, and so rises livingness in you.

In Plain English

Visualise — no, feel — light rising through your spine from the sex centre at the base to the crown at the top. As the light ascends through each energy centre, feel aliveness intensifying. You are not imagining something false; physics confirms you are made of light particles. This technique asks you to experience that truth directly.

How to Practice

  1. Preparation: Choose a time when you will not be disturbed for at least 45 minutes. Lock the door. Turn off your phone. This is non-negotiable — interruption mid-practice can leave energy stranded in uncomfortable places.
  2. Settle the body: Sit comfortably with spine erect, or lie down flat. Take 5–10 slow breaths. Let the body become heavy, grounded, still. Don’t begin the main practice until the surface mind has quieted.
  3. Develop feeling, not thinking: This is the critical distinction Osho emphasises. Do NOT think “light is rising.” Instead, FEEL warmth, tingling, aliveness. Start by feeling the warmth of your own body — the heat at your sex centre. That heat IS light slowed down. Let yourself feel it as luminous.
  4. First centre (Muladhara/sex centre): Feel a pool of warm golden light at the base of your spine. Don’t rush. Spend several minutes here until the warmth feels real. Let the warmth intensify naturally. It is not imagination — blood flows here, heat is real.
  5. Rising to the navel: When the base feels alive, allow the light to rise — like a slow river of warm honey — up through the belly to the navel centre. Feel the navel glow. Warmth spreads through the abdomen. The belly softens. Stay until it feels alive.
  6. Rising to the heart: Let the river of light continue upward to the heart. Here, something changes: breathing deepens spontaneously, the heartbeat may slow or become more noticeable. A feeling of openness, vulnerability, love may arise. Stay as long as the heart needs. Don’t push feelings away.
  7. Rising to the throat: The light ascends to the throat. You may feel a lump, a desire to speak or sing or cry. Let the throat open. The light purifies whatever is held here. Stay until it feels clear.
  8. Rising to the third eye: The light moves to the space between the eyebrows. Pressure may build here — this is normal. The inner visual field may shift: colours, patterns, or a sense of vast space. Stay until the energy feels ready to move further.
  9. Rising to the crown (sahasrar): Finally, feel the light push through the top of the head. Imagine — feel — the crown opening like a thousand-petalled lotus. Light streams upward into infinite space. You are not contained by the body any longer. Stay here as long as feels natural.
  10. Release into cosmos: Let the light dissolve upward into the sky. You are empty, transparent, clean. The “livingness” the sutra mentions is this: you feel more alive than you have ever felt, because the life-force is no longer blocked at any point.
  11. CRITICAL — Never leave incomplete: If you must stop early, ALWAYS bring the energy to the crown and release it upward before opening your eyes. Energy left at the sex centre or navel creates agitation; at the heart, emotional overwhelm; at the third eye, headaches. Complete the circuit.
  12. Return: When ready, feel the body again. Wiggle toes and fingers. Open eyes slowly. Sit for 2–3 minutes before standing. Drink water.

Questions From The Book

Why does Osho say this is not imagination but reality?

Chapter 46, "From the Beginning of Light"

Osho explains: modern physics confirms that all matter is condensed energy, and energy is light vibrating at different frequencies. Your body is literally made of light — photons, electrons, quanta of energy. When you “imagine” yourself as light, you are not adding something false; you are removing the false solidity that the mind projects. You are seeing what is actually there. The feeling confirms this — heat, tingling, aliveness — these are not hallucinations but direct perceptions of your own energy field.

What happens if energy gets stuck at a centre?

Chapter 46

Osho warns strongly: never leave this practice incomplete. If energy accumulates at the sex centre, you will feel extreme restlessness and sexual agitation. At the navel, digestive disturbance and anxiety. At the heart, uncontrollable emotions. At the throat, inability to sleep. At the third eye, splitting headaches. ALWAYS take the energy to the crown and release it. If you are interrupted, immediately visualise the light shooting up and out through the top of the head. This takes only seconds and prevents all problems.

Technique 71 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: lightning · jump · chakras · energy · spark
Or in the spaces between, feel this as lightning.

In Plain English

This is a variation of the previous technique. Instead of light rising gradually like a river, feel it JUMPING between centres like lightning bolts — instantaneous, electric, sharp. The spaces between the chakras are bridged in a flash. For minds that are naturally jumpy, restless, or male-energy-dominant, this abrupt approach may work better than the slow, gradual river of the previous sutra.

How to Practice

  1. Same preparation as Technique 1: Total privacy. 40–60 minutes of uninterrupted time. Spine erect or lying flat. 5–10 breaths to settle.
  2. Feel the base centre: Become aware of warmth and energy at the base of the spine. Let it gather intensity for 2–3 minutes — like a storm cloud building charge.
  3. FLASH to navel: In one instant — not gradually — feel the energy JUMP from base to navel. Like a lightning bolt. Like a spark leaping a gap. The sensation should be sudden, electric, vivid.
  4. Let the navel absorb: Pause at the navel for 1–2 minutes. Let the energy settle and intensify again. Build the charge.
  5. FLASH to heart: Another instantaneous jump. Base-to-navel was the first bolt; navel-to-heart is the second. Feel the electric shock of arrival at the heart.
  6. Continue upward: Heart to throat — flash. Throat to third eye — flash. Third eye to crown — the final bolt.
  7. At the crown: Feel all the lightning energy collecting and then bursting upward like a thunderbolt into the sky. Complete release.
  8. Rest in the aftermath: After the final release, sit in the profound silence that follows a storm. The body may feel electric, buzzing, incredibly alive.
  9. If too much heat arises: Osho warns that this technique can generate intense heat. If you feel burning, agitation, or overwhelm, SWITCH immediately to the gradual method (Technique 1). Move the energy slowly and gently to the crown. Not everyone is suited to the lightning approach.
  10. Experiment with both: Try the gradual method for a week. Then try the lightning method for a week. Your body will tell you which one is yours. Some people are rivers; some people are lightning. Neither is superior.

  • Same as Technique 1: NEVER leave incomplete. Always release at the crown.

Technique 72 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: translucence · cosmos · presence · sensitivity · perception
Feel the cosmos as a translucent ever-living presence.

In Plain English

Develop such sensitivity that you begin to perceive everything around you — trees, rocks, sky, other people — as luminous, translucent, alive with inner light. Not as dead matter but as living presence. This is not a belief to adopt but a perception to develop through months of patient practice.

How to Practice

  1. Phase 1 — Develop sensitivity (Week 1–2): Before attempting the main technique, spend two weeks developing raw sensitivity. Follow a child for an hour and notice how they touch everything. Touch your own body with awareness — feel the texture of your skin, the warmth. In the shower, feel every drop of water. Eat slowly, tasting fully. This phase is essential preparation.
  2. Phase 2 — Candle gazing (Week 3–4): Sit in a dark room with a single candle. Adopt a prayerful attitude — not religious prayer but an attitude of reverence and openness. Gaze at the flame for 5 minutes without blinking if possible. Notice: the flame is not one colour. As your eyes relax, you’ll see the full rainbow within it — blue at the base, white at the centre, gold and red at the edges. The flame is alive, dancing, ever-changing.
  3. Phase 3 — Seeing luminosity in objects (Week 5–8): After candle-gazing, look at other objects in the room. Carry the quality of the flame-gazing — that soft, receptive, reverent seeing — to everything. See the table as glowing from within. See the wall as translucent. Don’t THINK “this is translucent” — FEEL it. Let it happen.
  4. Phase 4 — Walking outside (Week 9–12): Take this perception outdoors. Walk slowly. See trees not as solid wood but as columns of living light. See the sky not as empty space but as luminous presence. See rocks not as dead matter but as condensed light vibrating slowly. See other people as flames walking around in body-shaped lanterns.
  5. The key instruction: Don’t think ABOUT translucence. Don’t philosophise. Don’t try to convince yourself. Just soften your gaze, open your feeling, and ALLOW the world to reveal its luminous nature. It will, if you are patient and sensitive enough.
  6. Duration: Practise 1 hour daily. The full transformation typically takes 3 months of consistent practice. Some moments of “seeing through” may come earlier as glimpses.
  7. Attitude: Approach with childlike wonder, not adult analysis. The child sees the world as magical because the child has not yet learned to see it as dead matter. You are unlearning deadness.
  8. Integration: Once this perception stabilises, it becomes your default way of seeing. The world never looks dead again. This is what mystics mean by “seeing God everywhere” — not a belief but a direct perception of aliveness.

Technique 73 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: sky · clarity · emptiness · open · staring
In summer when you see the entire sky endlessly clear, enter such clarity.

In Plain English

On a cloudless day, lie on your back and stare into the clear blue sky. Don’t think about the sky — enter it. Lose yourself in its boundlessness. When the outer sky fills you so completely that your inner space becomes sky-like, close your eyes — and discover the same infinite clarity within. The outer sky and the inner sky are one.

How to Practice

  1. Find your spot: A lonely beach, a rooftop, an open field, a garden — anywhere you can lie flat on your back with the full sky above you. The sky must be clear — cloudless, blue, boundless. Summer days are ideal.
  2. Lie down comfortably: Flat on your back, arms slightly away from the body, palms up. Let the body be completely relaxed. The ground holds you; you don’t need to hold yourself.
  3. Rule 1 — Don’t blink: Osho’s specific instruction. Keep your eyes open without blinking. Tears will come — this is good. The tears cleanse the eyes, and after the tears pass, vision becomes extraordinarily clear. The effort of not blinking keeps the mind from wandering.
  4. Rule 2 — Don’t think ABOUT the sky: Don’t say “how beautiful.” Don’t say “how vast.” Don’t analyse, don’t poeticise. Just stare. Let the sky pour into your eyes without commentary.
  5. Rule 3 — Stay at least 40 minutes: The mind will resist. At 5 minutes it will say “this is boring.” At 15 minutes it will create urgent thoughts. Stay. At 30–40 minutes, something shifts — the boundary between you and sky begins to dissolve.
  6. Enter the sky: Move deeper and deeper into the blue. Try to find its boundary — there is none. Try to find where it ends — it doesn’t. The boundlessness of the sky begins to infect your consciousness. Your mind, which is normally full of boundaries, starts to become boundless.
  7. The merging: At some point, “you” looking at “sky” becomes simply... sky. The watcher and the watched merge. This may come as a sudden shift or a gradual dissolution. Either way, it is unmistakable.
  8. Close your eyes: When the merging happens (or after 40 minutes), gently close your eyes. Now look within. The same sky is here — vast, clear, boundless, empty. The inner sky meets the outer sky. They were never separate.
  9. Alternative for cloudy days: If no clear sky is available, close your eyes and focus on the SPACE between thoughts. Not the thoughts themselves — the gaps. Those gaps are the inner sky. They are just as vast, just as clear, just as boundless. Rest in the gaps.
  10. Return: When you feel ready, bring attention back to the body. Feel the ground beneath you. Sit up slowly. The clarity of the sky often remains for hours afterward.

Technique 74 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: space · head · absorption · vastness · expansion
Shakti, see all space as if already absorbed in your own head in the brilliance.

In Plain English

Expand your sense of self until all space — the room, the city, the sky, the cosmos — is contained within your own head. Not that you are in space, but that space is in you. This is the most powerful and potentially overwhelming technique in this category. The mind, which needs narrowness to exist, cannot survive when all space enters it. The mind dissolves; only brilliance remains.

How to Practice

  1. CAUTION: This technique should be done with a companion present (in the next room or nearby). It can produce dizziness, disorientation, or loss of consciousness in some practitioners. Do NOT attempt this alone until you have done it successfully with support at least 5–10 times.
  2. Week 1 — Small expansion: Lie in bed before sleep. Feel your body as normal. Then imagine — FEEL — your body extending 6 inches beyond its physical boundary in every direction. Feel your feet 6 inches longer, your head 6 inches taller, your body 6 inches wider. Just 6 inches. Get comfortable with this small expansion. Practice nightly for a week.
  3. Week 2 — Room-sized: Same practice, but now expand until you FEEL 12 feet tall, filling the entire room. The walls are inside you. The ceiling is inside your head. The floor is inside your feet. Don’t think it — FEEL it. Your body-sense expands to fill the room.
  4. Week 3 — House-sized: Expand further. Feel the whole house within you. Other rooms, other people — all inside your expanded body-sense. The roof is inside your head. The garden is inside your belly.
  5. Week 4 — City-sized and beyond: Continue expanding. The city is inside you. The countryside. The continent. The earth. The sky. Eventually: ALL SPACE is absorbed in your own head. You are not inside space — space is inside you.
  6. The critical moment: When space truly enters your head, the mind disappears. This is because the mind needs narrowness, confinement, boundaries to function. When all boundaries are gone — when infinity is inside you — the mind has no walls to bounce between. It collapses. What remains is “brilliance” — pure awareness without form.
  7. The impact is tremendous: Osho warns that this can be overwhelming. Some people faint. Some feel terror. Some experience ecstasy. This is why a companion is necessary — someone to bring you water, to ground you, to simply be present so you are not alone if the experience becomes too intense.
  8. Grounding afterward: After each session, deliberately contract back to body-size. Feel your physical boundaries. Stamp your feet. Drink cold water. Touch something solid. Don’t drive or operate machinery for at least 30 minutes after deep practice.
  9. Build over 3 months: This is NOT a technique to rush. Each week’s expansion must be stable before moving to the next. If any stage produces too much discomfort, stay at that stage until it becomes comfortable. There is no prize for speed.
  10. Group practice: Osho recommends doing this in a group of 3–5 people, all practising simultaneously. The shared field supports the expansion and provides safety.

Technique 75 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: light · flame · waking · sleeping · dreaming · continuity
Waking, sleeping, dreaming, know you as light.

In Plain English

Carry a single perception — that you are a flame of light — through all three states of consciousness: waking, dreaming, and deep sleep. Start with waking only. When the flame becomes stable enough that it persists into dreams, carry it deeper into dreamless sleep. When you remain aware through all three states, you become the fourth — turiya — the witness that never sleeps.

How to Practice

  1. Phase 1 — Waking state (Months 1–3): Throughout your day — while walking, eating, working, talking — maintain the feeling that a small, bright flame is burning in your heart centre. The body is merely the aura around this flame — a transparent glass chimney. You are not a solid physical body with a soul somewhere inside; you are a flame with a body-shaped glow around it.
  2. Make it felt, not thought: Don’t keep repeating “I am light.” Instead, FEEL the warmth at the heart. Feel the luminosity. Feel the lightness (both less-heavy and more-luminous). Let it become your background body-sense.
  3. Persist for 3 months: This is not a 20-minute meditation — it is a 24/7 remembrance during waking hours. At first you’ll forget constantly. That’s fine. Each time you remember, gently return to the flame. Over weeks, the gaps between forgetting shorten.
  4. The sign that Phase 1 is complete: You feel a continuous warmth in the heart area throughout the day without needing to remind yourself. The flame has become a background presence. When the remembrance has become effortless and nearly constant during waking hours, you are ready to proceed.
  5. Phase 2 — Dreaming state: Only after Phase 1 is stable. As you fall asleep each night, feel the flame at the heart. Let it be the last thing in awareness as you drift off. The flame will begin to appear in your dreams — sometimes as a literal light, sometimes as an unusual clarity within the dream. Eventually, the flame’s presence in dreams gives you lucidity — you know you are dreaming.
  6. When dreams disappear: As the flame becomes constant in dreams, something remarkable happens: dreams stop. Not because you are suppressing them, but because the flame’s light dissolves the dream-material. Dreams are made of unconsciousness; when light enters, they evaporate.
  7. Phase 3 — Deep sleep: Only when dreams have largely disappeared. Now the flame persists even in deep sleep. You are no longer unconscious during sleep — a witness remains, aware of the darkness, aware of the body resting, aware of the gap. This is turiya — the fourth state.
  8. Timeline: This is a long-term practice. Phase 1 alone takes 3 months minimum. The full practice may take a year or more. This is not a quick technique — it is a life-practice.
  9. Don’t skip phases: Each phase must be stable before moving to the next. If the waking-state flame is not yet noticed by others, the dreaming phase will not work. If dreams have not dissolved, deep-sleep awareness will not arise. Trust the sequence.
  10. The result: When turiya is established, you never truly sleep again — the body sleeps but you remain aware. This is not insomnia; the body gets full rest. But consciousness never switches off. You become continuous awareness — 24/7, unbroken, the same light in waking, dreaming, and deep sleep.

Technique 76 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: darkness · night · form · formlessness · surrender
In rain during a black night, enter that blackness as the form of forms.

In Plain English

On a moonless, rainy night — when darkness is absolute — go outside with open eyes and stare into that blackness. Not the blackness behind closed eyelids (that is merely the negative of light) but real, external, total darkness. Let the darkness enter your eyes. Let yourself dissolve into it. The darkness is not empty — it is the “form of forms,” the womb from which all creation arises. Enter it as you would enter a mother’s embrace.

How to Practice

  1. Phase 1 — Overcome fear of darkness (Weeks 1–2): Most people have a deep, unconscious fear of darkness. Before attempting the main technique, befriend the dark. Sit in your room with all lights off for 30 minutes nightly. Don’t meditate — just BE in the dark. Let your eyes adjust. Let the fear surface, be felt, and pass. Do this until darkness feels comfortable, even inviting.
  2. Phase 2 — Develop love for darkness: Begin to notice what darkness gives you that light cannot: rest for the eyes, cool relaxation, mystery, womb-like safety, the dissolution of visual boundaries. Let yourself LOVE the dark — as a friend, as a mother, as the original state from which you came.
  3. Phase 3 — The main practice: Wait for a moonless, rainy night. Rain is specified because rain clouds block all starlight, making the darkness truly total. Also, the sound of rain relaxes the mind and provides a gentle background for surrender.
  4. Go outside: Stand or sit in a safe, open area. Keep your eyes OPEN — this is crucial. You are not looking at closed-eyelid darkness (which is just retinal noise) but at actual, external, absolute darkness. There is nothing to see — and that “nothing” becomes everything.
  5. Let darkness enter your eyes: Normally you project outward through the eyes — looking AT things. Now reverse it. Let the darkness come IN through the eyes. Be receptive. Your eyes are doors — normally you go out through them; now let the dark come in. Tears may come; the eyes may hurt slightly. Persist.
  6. Enter the darkness: When real darkness has entered you, a shift happens: YOU enter IT. The boundary between inside and outside dissolves. You are no longer a person surrounded by darkness. You ARE the darkness. There is no separation.
  7. Feel darkness as the cosmic womb: Lie down if you can (dry shelter nearby if raining heavily). Feel yourself held by infinite blackness as a baby is held in the womb. There is nothing to do, nothing to be, no identity needed in total darkness. The ego cannot exist without light to reflect off of. In absolute dark, you become formless — and that formlessness is peace.
  8. Carry a patch of darkness: After the session, as you return to light, try to carry a patch of that inner darkness with you. Throughout the next day, remember the black velvet quality behind your solar plexus. Osho says: those who carry darkness within become deeply relaxed, cool, passionless, free of anger and agitation.
  9. Historical note: The Essenes — an ancient mystery school — meditated on God as absolute darkness, not light. Their reasoning: light is created; darkness is uncreated. Darkness was before “let there be light.” Darkness IS the ultimate formlessness from which all forms emerge.
  10. Duration: 1 hour sessions, practised for 3 months for full transformation. Weekly sessions on dark nights as available.

Technique 77 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: darkness · inner · faults · purification · eyes
When a moonless rainy night is not present, close eyes and find blackness before you. Opening eyes, see blackness. So faults disappear forever.

In Plain English

This is the indoor version of the previous technique — for when actual darkness is unavailable. Close your eyes and find the natural blackness that is there. Then open your eyes and PROJECT that inner darkness outward — see blackness even with eyes open. When inner and outer darkness merge into totality, all “faults” (disturbances, impurities, egoic patterns) disappear — because in total darkness there is no ego, no identity, no separation between self and other.

How to Practice

  1. Find a quiet space: Any room, any time. Dim the lights if possible, but this technique works even in a lit room because you are working with inner darkness projected outward.
  2. Close your eyes: Look at the blackness that appears. Don’t look for colours, patterns, or images — focus on the BLACK itself. The darkness behind your eyelids. Let it deepen, thicken, become velvety.
  3. Stay with the blackness for 5–10 minutes: Let it become rich, deep, total. If colours or images appear, gently return attention to the black background behind them. The darkness is always there behind everything else.
  4. Open your eyes: Now, keeping that quality of inner darkness alive, open your eyes. See the outer world but MAINTAIN the sense of darkness. Project the inner blackness outward. Let it overlay what you see. The room may appear darker, edges may soften, the visual field may seem less solid.
  5. Merge inner and outer: The inner darkness (eyes closed) and the outer seeing (eyes open) are not two different things. Let them merge. The darkness is everywhere — behind appearances, between objects, within objects. Everything floats in darkness.
  6. Alternate: Close eyes (find blackness) → open eyes (maintain blackness) → close eyes (deepen) → open eyes (maintain). Each cycle, the darkness becomes more stable and pervasive even with eyes open.
  7. “Faults disappear”: Osho explains that “faults” here means disturbances, impurities, mental agitations. In total darkness there is no ego — because ego needs light (reflection, visibility, comparison) to exist. When you carry total darkness even with eyes open, the ego has no mirror. Without a mirror, it cannot exist. Without ego, its faults (anger, greed, jealousy) have no home.
  8. Practice duration: 20–30 minutes per session. Can be done daily without special conditions.

Technique 78 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: attention · presence · immediacy · this-point · awareness
Wherever your attention alights, at this very point, experience.

In Plain English

This is perhaps the simplest and most profound technique in the entire light category — and it is disguised as nothing special. Wherever your attention goes — to a sound, a colour, a face, a sensation, a thought — at THAT very point, be completely present. Don’t move on. Don’t interpret. Don’t compare. Just EXPERIENCE the raw aliveness of whatever attention touches. The point of attention becomes luminous when you are fully there. The “light” in this technique is the light of awareness itself.

How to Practice

  1. No special conditions needed: This can be done anywhere, anytime, in any posture, with eyes open or closed. It requires nothing except total presence.
  2. Notice where your attention goes: Right now, something is attracting your attention — these words, a background sound, a body sensation, a thought. Whatever it is, DON’T interfere with it. Let attention go where it naturally goes.
  3. At THAT point, be totally present: The key word is “at this very point.” Don’t move to the next thing. Don’t label it. Don’t think about it. Just BE with it. Experience it raw, naked, without the layer of interpretation.
  4. The point becomes luminous: When you are fully present with anything — a sound, a colour, a texture, even a thought — it begins to glow with aliveness. This is not hallucination; it is what happens when attention is undivided. Normally attention is scattered; when collected at one point, it creates a quality of brilliance at that point.
  5. Don’t hold or grasp: When attention naturally moves to something else, let it. But at the NEW point, be fully there again. The practice is not concentration (forcing attention to stay) but presence (being total wherever attention happens to be).
  6. Formal practice (20 min): Sit quietly. Let attention wander freely. But wherever it alights — on a breath, a sound, an itch, a memory — be completely there for as long as it stays. When it moves, follow it with the same totality. Every point of attention becomes a meditation object.
  7. Informal practice (all day): Throughout the day, periodically notice: where is my attention right now? At that point, be fully present for a moment. Then continue with your activity. This takes zero time out of your day but transforms the quality of every moment.
  8. The insight: You will discover that what we call “light” or “consciousness” or “awareness” is simply what happens when attention is total. There is no separate mystical light to seek — the light IS attention itself, fully present. Every moment is already luminous; you just haven’t been fully there to notice.
  9. Don’t make it complicated: This technique is so simple that the mind refuses to believe it can work. The mind wants something complex, esoteric, special. But the sutra is clear: wherever attention alights, at THIS VERY POINT, experience. That’s all. Nothing more is needed.
  10. Deepening: Over time, the gaps between moments of presence shrink. You spend more and more of the day actually HERE rather than lost in commentary about here. This is the gradual illumination of life itself — not through special states but through ordinary attention made total.

Technique 79 of 112
Category: Light & Gazing · Tags: fire · burning · body · ashes · transcendence
Focus on fire rising through your form from the toes up until the body burns to ashes but not you.

In Plain English

Imagine fire starting at your toes and rising upward through the body. The flesh burns. The bones turn to ash. The entire body is consumed by flames. But YOU remain — the witness, the awareness that cannot burn. What the fire destroys is everything you are NOT. What survives the fire is what you ARE. This is a death meditation using fire as the vehicle of dissolution.

How to Practice

  1. Sit or lie in complete stillness: The body must be absolutely motionless for this practice to work deeply. Any movement breaks the spell. Settle in for 30 minutes. Resolve not to move.
  2. Close your eyes. Relax completely: Take 5 slow breaths. Feel the weight of the body. Feel its solidity. Feel its heaviness. You are about to let all of that burn.
  3. Ignite the fire at the toes: Imagine — FEEL — fire beginning at your toes. Not a gentle warmth but actual fire. Flames licking at the flesh. The toes begin to burn. Feel the heat, the consuming, the dissolution.
  4. Rise slowly upward: Let the fire travel upward: feet burning, ankles, calves, knees. The flesh chars and turns to ash. The bones glow orange and crumble. Move slowly — spend at least 15 minutes on the journey from toes to head.
  5. Through the torso: Fire reaches the belly. The organs burn — intestines, liver, stomach, all consumed. The spine glows and crumbles. The heart burns last in the chest — feel it catch fire and dissolve.
  6. The head: Fire reaches the neck, the jaw, the face. Eyes burn. Brain burns. The skull cracks in the heat and crumbles to ash. Everything is gone. The entire body is now a pile of ash — or simply empty space where a body used to be.
  7. But NOT you: This is the crucial point of the sutra. The body has burned completely. But you — the one who watched the burning — remain. You cannot burn. You were never the body. You are the awareness in which the body appeared and in which it has now disappeared. THAT is you.
  8. Rest in what remains: After the body is gone, rest in the pure awareness that survives. It has no form, no location, no weight, no boundaries. It simply IS. Stay here for 5–10 minutes.
  9. This is a death meditation: You are rehearsing death — not morbidly but as a way of discovering what cannot die. Osho says: if you can “die” in meditation and find what survives, you will never fear physical death again. You already know what you are — and it is not the body.
  10. Return: Slowly, rebuild the sense of the body. Feel it reconstituting from the ashes — or simply open your eyes and feel the body is still here. But now you know: the body is what you HAVE, not what you ARE.
  11. Frequency: Once daily for 30 minutes. Particularly powerful when done in the evening before sleep, as it creates a quality of letting-go that deepens sleep significantly.
  12. Variation: If fire feels too violent, you can use water (body dissolving into ocean) or air (body evaporating into sky). But fire is the most effective because it is the most total — nothing remains after fire but ash.

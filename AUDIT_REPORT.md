# Fidelity audit: AI-generated technique pages vs. Osho's *The Book of Secrets*

**Files audited (alphabetical, first five):**
1. `vigyan-bhairav-breath-techniques.html` — 8 techniques
2. `vigyan-bhairav-centering-techniques.html` — 10 techniques
3. `vigyan-bhairav-death-techniques.html` — 4 techniques
4. `vigyan-bhairav-heart-techniques.html` — 5 techniques
5. `vigyan-bhairav-imagination-techniques.html` — 6 techniques

**Method.** I extracted the full text of *The Book of Secrets* (the EPUB in this repo). For each technique I located the matching sutra in Osho's chapters by grepping a distinctive phrase, then read the surrounding 200–600 lines of his commentary, then compared line-by-line against the AI-written *How to Practice*, *Signs It's Working*, and *Common Pitfalls* sections.

**Verdict scale used.**
- **Faithful** — corresponds to something Osho actually says about *that* sutra.
- **Extrapolated** — not stated by Osho but a defensible inference from his teaching.
- **Fabricated** — modern coaching language / generic mindfulness platitudes / invented phenomenology not in Osho.
- **Contradicts** — actively reverses or violates what Osho says.

---

## Headline pattern (true of all five files)

There is a structural problem with the page format itself, not just individual entries:

- **"How to Practice" is mostly OK** — usually a defensible operationalisation of the sutra, though it routinely sanitises Osho's more uncompromising instructions and inserts modern conveniences (specific durations, "best time," "best for" labels).
- **"Signs It's Working" is overwhelmingly fabricated.** Osho almost never gives bullet-point phenomenological checklists for any technique. He talks about results in image-rich, paradoxical, or theological language. The AI repeatedly produces lists of somatic markers ("warm tingles," "click," "drop feeling," "tears without sadness") that are not in Osho's commentary on the corresponding sutra.
- **"Common Pitfalls" is mixed.** A few items per file are genuine Osho warnings; the majority are modern meditation-coaching extensions — useful, but presented as if from Osho.
- **Several sutras themselves have been silently re-worded by the AI.** This is the biggest fidelity problem: in some cases the AI has altered the sutra text and then written commentary on the altered version, so even a faithful-looking page is built on a non-Osho foundation.

## File 1 — Breath techniques (8 techniques)

Sources: Osho Chapter 3 ("Breath – A Bridge to the Universe") and Chapter 5 ("Five Techniques of Attentiveness").

### Sutras
All 8 sutras match Osho's text accurately.

### Worst case: Technique 4 ("breath stopped of itself")
- **Osho says explicitly: "It is difficult for you; you cannot practice it. But you can feel it sometimes suddenly."**
- He gives examples of *spontaneous* stoppage: a near-accident, Zen masters slapping disciples without warning, a city stunned by Shiva's marriage procession.
- The AI's "How to Practice" turns it into a regular sit-and-watch routine: "Lie down. Take a few slow, full breaths to settle… After a normal exhale, sometimes the breath does not immediately return. There is a small 'settle' before the in-breath comes. Watch for that."
- **Verdict: How to Practice — CONTRADICTS Osho.** This technique is, in Osho's framing, only available to "the pure" or in shock moments — not to a beginner deciding to practice it for 15 minutes after dinner.

### Best case: Technique 5 ("Attention between eyebrows")
- The "How to Practice" steps closely match Osho's actual instructions: focus eyes between the eyebrows, watch them lock, witness thoughts as on a film screen, then imagine breath-essence rising and showering as light from the crown.
- "Signs It's Working" includes two **directly faithful** signs:
  - "Eyes lock at the third-eye point and feel reluctant to move" — Osho: *"suddenly your eyes will become fixed; they cannot move."*
  - "Thoughts appear as separate from you, like film on a screen" — Osho: *"It is just like a film screen: thoughts are running and you are a witness."*
- "Common Pitfalls" contains one **directly faithful** warning:
  - "Practising with an unsettled mind — the sutra's old commentary insists on purity first because at the third eye, imagination becomes unusually potent" — almost verbatim Osho: *"there is so much insistence on purity… because if you are focused at the third eye and your mind is impure, your imagination can become dangerous."*

### Most distorted in framing: Technique 6 ("worldly activity, attention between two breaths")
- Osho's central teaching for this sutra is that it **turns life into a drama**: "Your activity will become an acting, as if you are playing a part… you will have two layers of existence – doing and being… your whole life will be as if it is not happening to you."
- The AI's version reduces this to generic mindfulness coaching: "Routine tasks start feeling oddly fresh… the sense of 'rushing through' the day weakens… reactions to small irritations soften."
- **Verdict: How to Practice — extrapolated; Signs It's Working — fabricated; the drama/play framing that is Osho's central image is missing.**

### Other techniques (1, 2, 3, 7, 8) — How to Practice
Mostly extrapolated-but-defensible operationalisations of the sutra. Specific items the AI invents that are not in Osho:
- All quantitative durations ("20 min", "15-20 min", "20-30 min").
- All "Best for" / "Avoid if" labels.
- The progression order (Tech 4 requires "few months in 1-3", etc.) — Osho does not establish this internal sequence.
- Tech 8 ("With utmost devotion"): the AI says "bring to mind whatever moves you most — a beloved figure, the open sky, the felt sense of life itself." Osho's specific framing is theological: *"the body is the temple… it is not you who is taking the breath, it is the divine within you."* The AI secularises Osho's bhakti language.

### Signs It's Working — fabrication-rate by technique
| Technique | Signs given | In Osho | Fabricated/extrapolated |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (gap after in-breath) | 4 | 0 | 4 |
| 2 (both turning points) | 3 | 0 | 3 |
| 3 (fusion at navel) | 3 | 0 | 3 |
| 4 (universal pause) | 3 | 0 | 3 |
| 5 (third eye) | 4 | 2 | 2 |
| 6 (worldly activity) | 4 | 0 | 4 |

### Common Pitfalls — fabrication-rate by technique
Mostly modern meditation-coaching extensions ("counting breaths turns meditation into arithmetic", "remembering is the practice; forgetting just makes remembering possible", "treating boredom as failure"). One genuinely Osho-derived warning: Tech 5's purity warning. One actively contradicts Osho: Tech 6's "Trying to maintain it 100% — a few seconds an hour is more than most adults manage; that is enough" — Osho specifically says "*This sixth technique has to be done continuously.*"

## File 2 — Centering techniques (10 techniques)

Sources: Osho Chapters 9, 11, 13, 14, 20, 47.

### Sutra accuracy
- t1 ("translucent ever-living presence") — matches Osho exactly.
- t2 ("moving vehicle, by rhythmically swaying…") — matches.
- t3 (peacock-tail / point) — matches but the AI has cut the peacock imagery in half ("Choose any small point — a dot on the wall, a candle flame, or even an imagined point").
- t4 (spinal column lotus thread) — matches.
- t5 (closing the seven openings) — matches.
- **t6 — partially altered.** Osho's actual sutra: *"Blessed one, as senses are absorbed in the heart, reach the center of the lotus."* AI has rewritten it as: *"Let your senses be absorbed into your heart. Let the centre of your being be the centre of all your senses."* The "centre of all your senses" sentence is AI-added.
- t7 ("Unminding mind, keep in the middle — until.") — matches.
- t8 ("Sit only on the buttocks…") — matches.
- t9 ("Pierce some part of your nectar-filled form with a pin…") — matches.
- t10 ("gradually being deprived of strength or knowledge") — matches.

### Worst case: Technique 8 ("Sit only on the buttocks")
The AI describes a **completely different physical technique** from what Osho describes.

- **Osho:** Sit in lotus / siddhasana / any seated asana. Hands NOT supporting weight. Then notice which buttock is bearing more weight; balance the weight evenly between the two. *"When your buttocks are sensitive this will not be difficult… both buttocks are feeling the same. Your weight on both of the buttocks is exactly the same."* He prefaces this by saying the practitioner must first spend time making the buttocks sensitive (touching them to walls, beds, partner). This is essentially "Buddha's seated posture, explained as a buttock-balancing meditation."
- **AI:** "Lean back slightly and lift both feet off the ground. Arms can extend for balance or rest on knees. Find the tipping point where you're balanced solely on the sit-bones." This is a *navasana* / boat-pose / V-sit. Feet are off the floor, abs engaged.
- **Verdict: How to Practice — CONTRADICTS Osho.** Two completely different physical actions. The AI also omits the prerequisite of cultivating buttock-sensitivity (which is the bulk of Osho's instruction for this technique).

### Second-worst case: Technique 5 ("Closing the seven openings")
- **Osho:** *"Do not practice it – only suddenly is it helpful… When you feel suffocated, go on – unless it becomes absolutely unbearable… If you can persist in that moment, suddenly everything will be illumined."* Crucially, Osho repeatedly says: "do not practice it daily; if you practice it daily, then nothing will happen — it becomes a mechanical habit."
- **AI:** Step 6: "**Breathe softly through the slight gap in the nostrils. Don't hold your breath.**" Step 8: "Stay for 5–15 minutes. Release when ready."
- The AI converts this into the standard yogic *shanmukhi mudra* — a regular calm pranayama. Osho's version is closer to a controlled near-suffocation that *breaks* the breath/mind association.
- **Verdict: How to Practice — CONTRADICTS Osho** ("Don't hold your breath" is the exact opposite of the instruction).

### Significant inversion: Technique 10 ("gradually being deprived")
- **Osho:** Lie down and feel "I am dying, I am dying, I am dying." Body becomes **heavy as lead**. *"You will feel as if your body has become like lead. You want to move your hand, but you cannot move; it has become so heavy and dead."* When body feels dead → witness it as a corpse → transcend.
- **AI:** "Slowly imagine the weight dissolving — feet becoming weightless, then legs, hips, torso… Feel the entire body as **light, floating, almost not there**."
- The AI inverts the direction. Osho: become heavy/dead. AI: become light/floating. The AI's version is essentially yoga-nidra body-scan; Osho's is a death-rehearsal.
- **Verdict: How to Practice — CONTRADICTS Osho on the core felt sense.**

### Subtle inversion: Technique 2 ("moving vehicle, swaying")
- **Osho:** For the still-room version, "First take a **big** circle, then go on slowing it… slowing, slowing, **making it smaller and smaller and smaller**, until your body is not visibly moving." The Gurdjieff/Sufi whirling reference is central.
- **AI:** "Gently initiate **small** circular swaying yourself, then let the circles slow down until they stop." Starts small instead of big. The Gurdjieff/Sufi reference is omitted.
- **Verdict: How to Practice — partially contradicts on the "start big, slow down" mechanic that gives the technique its momentum.**

### Other techniques (1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 9) — How to Practice
- **t1 (translucent cosmos)** — extrapolated and very secular. Osho's source chapter places this in a much heavier context ("a translucent ever-living presence" is treated as a perception of the divine). The AI's instructions ("Look at whatever is in front of you — don't label it") are reasonable but strip the metaphysics.
- **t3 (point/peacock)** — partially faithful but **contradicts Osho on a key instruction**. The AI says: "Eyes may water — that's fine" / Pitfall: "blink when you must; the point is a focus, not a punishment." Osho says: *"Do not blink your eyes, because blinking gives a space for the mind to move again. Do not blink."* He even tells the Bodhidharma story (master tearing off his own eyelids to stop blinking, origin of tea) to drive the point home. The AI inverts this.
- **t4 (spinal column / lotus thread)** — extrapolated. Osho's instruction is to **visualise** the spinal column and a **silver cord / lotus thread inside it**. The AI says "feel the spine as a thread of light," which omits the inner-thread teaching that is the whole point. Pitfall: "Confusing visualisation with feeling — feel the spine, don't draw a mental picture" — this directly contradicts Osho who says "visualize" repeatedly.
- **t6 (senses absorbed in heart)** — extrapolated. Osho's specific examples (touch a tree from the heart; listen to music headlessly; *"The heart is the lotus. Every sense is just the petals"*) are replaced by the AI's "lake-and-rivers" metaphor.
- **t7 (Unminding mind, keep in the middle)** — faithful in spirit. Captures the Buddha middle-way teaching. **Misses** Osho's most concrete and counter-intuitive instruction: that after anger you must NOT repent, because repentance is the opposite pole that energises the next anger. The AI's version is more abstract ("don't follow preferences").
- **t9 (pierce with a pin)** — sanitised and partially mis-framed. Osho's instruction is literal: have a friend pierce your back with a pin (avoiding "blind spots"); enter the piercing as a witness. The AI's pitfall says: *"the sutra uses 'pin' as a metaphor; do NOT injure yourself."* This is **incorrect about Osho** — he is not metaphorical. He literally says "*give a pin to anyone, to your friend, and sit, and tell your friend to pierce your back with the pin at many points.*" The AI's safety-first sanitisation is a defensible modern move but it misrepresents Osho.

### Signs It's Working across centering
Same pattern as breath: ~85–90% fabricated. Specific Osho-faithful items I found:
- t4 spinal column: nothing about tingles/warmth/expansion in Osho. Osho's actual sign is that *"auras are seen"* — the AI doesn't mention auras.
- t5 seven openings: Osho's actual sign is the Mansoor-style realisation "I am God / aham brahmasmi" — the AI mentions inner hum, warmth, vast cave. Modern phenomenology.
- t6 heart-absorption: nothing in Osho.
- t7 unminding-middle: nothing in Osho's specific commentary.

### Common Pitfalls across centering
Mostly fabricated coaching. Three real findings:
- **t3 (point) — pitfall directly contradicts Osho** ("blink when you must" vs. Osho's "do not blink").
- **t4 (spinal column) — pitfall partially contradicts Osho** ("Confusing visualisation with feeling — feel the spine, don't draw a mental picture") vs. Osho: *"close your eyes and visualize his spinal column."*
- **t9 (pierce) — pitfall mis-frames Osho** ("the sutra uses 'pin' as a metaphor"; Osho is literal).

## File 3 — Death techniques (4 techniques)

Sources: Osho Chapter 38 (file _42_), Chapter 65 (file _59_).

### Sutra accuracy
- **t1 ("Focus on fire rising through your form…")** — matches Osho exactly.
- **t2 ("Imagine spirit simultaneously within and around you…")** — matches Osho exactly.
- **t3 ("In the certainty that the impermanent shall pass, rest in the permanent.")** — **NOT FOUND verbatim in Osho's commentary.** Osho discusses impermanence in many places (Buddha's dirty-stream story, etc.), but this is not one of the original 112 sutras as quoted in his book. It looks like an AI paraphrase or an outright synthetic sutra.
- **t4 ("Just as you have the intuition that something will happen, the very moment you feel it will happen — stop at this point.")** — **NOT FOUND verbatim in Osho's commentary.** I searched several phrasings ("intuition," "stop at this point," "the moment you feel it will happen"). The "stop techniques" cluster (Osho's Chapter 17, "Several Stop Techniques") covers the closely related sutras "Whenever an action begins, stop. Then no longer doing nor having a thought, leap into being" and "Just as you start a sneeze or before, stop." The AI's t4 is a paraphrastic blend.

### Technique 1 ("Focus on fire") — How to Practice
- **Osho's actual prerequisites** that the AI omits:
  - Watch a real cremation — Buddha required disciples to do this for **3 months** before practising. Osho says: *"if you want to do this technique, go to the burning place. Watch — not for three months — but at least watch one body being burned; observe it."*
  - Throw away the fear of death first.
  - Pre-meditation of **15 minutes of deep exhalation** (not inhalation), because *"if you can pay all attention to exhalation… you will have a taste of [death]."*
- **Osho's actual practice** (very simple):
  1. Lie down. Conceive of yourself as dead.
  2. Bring attention to the toes. Start fire there. *Why toes?* Because they are *"the most far away point from the ego"* (which lives in the head).
  3. Watch the fire rise upward; each part turns to ash.
  4. End at the head. Become "a watcher on the hill."
- **Osho's diagnostic for whether it worked:** *"if the memory is still there, then the body remains and you have been playing tricks. If really you go deep in the feeling that the body is dead, burning, and the fire has completely destroyed it, you will not have any memory in that moment."*
- **AI version:** 10 elaborate steps with detailed sensory narration ("the skin curling, the flesh dissolving, the bones glowing red then crumbling… the brain burns — all memories, all knowledge, all personality crumbles to ash"). The visceral elaboration is the AI's invention. Then: *"Return with knowledge: When ready, let the body reform in awareness. Feel it reconstituting — like putting on a garment."* This "reconstitution" stage is **not in Osho** — Osho ends with the watcher recognising the absent ego, not with reassembling the body.
- **Verdict: How to Practice — extrapolated, with significant added narrative content and significant omitted prerequisites.**

### Technique 1 ("Focus on fire") — Signs It's Working
- "Genuine physical sensations of heat" — not in Osho.
- "Lightness and weightlessness as body parts burn away" — not in Osho.
- "I am still here even though the body is gone" — **faithful** (this is Osho's "watcher on the hill" insight).
- "Body feeling like a garment rather than an identity" — modern coaching language; Osho doesn't use the garment image here.
- "Deeper appreciation for life arising from confronting impermanence" — modern coaching, not in Osho.
- **Verdict: Signs — mostly fabricated, with one faithful sign.**

### Technique 1 ("Focus on fire") — Common Pitfalls
- "Rushing the burning" — not in Osho (he gives no time prescription). Reasonable inference.
- "Keeping it purely visual without FEELING it" — partially aligned (Osho emphasises authentic feeling, not just visualisation, but he also says "visualize") — minor extrapolation.
- "Panicking when the head/brain burns" — not in Osho. Reasonable inference.
- "Skipping to the deathless insight without fully completing the burning" — sequencing advice. Inference.
- "Using as mere relaxation rather than genuine death rehearsal" — not in Osho but very compatible with his framing.
- **Verdict: Pitfalls — extrapolated. None contradict Osho.**

### Technique 2 ("Imagine spirit simultaneously within and around")
- **How to Practice:** 10 steps, expanding awareness from inside body → body surface → around body → room → universe. The progression is plausible and broadly compatible with the sutra. Osho's commentary on this sutra (Chapter 65, "Watch from the Hill") discusses how when you feel yourself as the entire field, "the wave dies; the ocean remains." The AI says the same in different words. **Verdict: extrapolated.** No specific Osho instruction is contradicted, but the 10-step "expand by stages" procedure is the AI's structure.
- **Signs:** Generic expansion-meditation phenomenology. **Mostly fabricated.**
- **Pitfalls:** Modern coaching ("Creating a grandiose ego 'I am the universe!'", "Confusing dissociation with expansion"). Reasonable, not in Osho. **Extrapolated.**

### Technique 3 ("impermanent shall pass, rest in the permanent") — entire commentary suspect
The sutra itself does not appear verbatim as one of the 112 in Osho's book. This means **the entire How to Practice / Signs / Pitfalls block is the AI writing about an AI-created (or AI-paraphrased) sutra**, not commenting on a teaching Osho gave. **Verdict: SUTRA NOT VERBATIM IN OSHO. Treat the whole entry as AI-authored.**

### Technique 4 ("intuition that something will happen, stop at this point") — sutra suspect
Same problem as t3. The AI's sutra is a paraphrase/blend that does not match Osho's "stop techniques" sutras. **Verdict: SUTRA NOT VERBATIM IN OSHO.**

## File 4 — Heart techniques (5 techniques)

Sources: Osho Chapters 19/20, 31, 35.

### Sutra accuracy — this file has the worst record on sutras
- **t1 ("At the point of sleep…")** — matches Osho exactly. Located in Chapter 35 ("Turning Inward toward the Real").
- **t2 ("Devotion frees.")** — matches Osho exactly. Located in Chapter 19/20.
- **t3 ("Feel love filling you from all sides, and become love itself.")** — **NOT FOUND verbatim in Osho.** No matching sutra. Likely AI-invented in this exact wording.
- **t4 ("As you sing, or listen to music, enter the feeling totally — become the feeling.")** — **NOT FOUND verbatim.** This is a synthetic line that conflates several distinct sound/music sutras Osho actually quotes ("Bathe in the centre of sound," "While listening to stringed instruments, hear their composite central sound," "In the beginning and gradual refinement of the sound of any letter, awake"). The AI flattens three or four specific techniques into one feel-good paraphrase.
- **t5 ("Wherever your mind is settled, feel this moment as the eternal.")** — **the sutra has been altered.** Osho's actual sutra (from Chapter 31) reads: *"Wherever your mind is wandering, internally or externally, at this very place, this."* The AI changed "**wandering**" → "**settled**" (opposite meaning) and added the "feel this moment as the eternal" framing, which is not in the sutra. The AI also claims this is "technique 112 of 112 — the final sutra in the Vigyan Bhairav Tantra." It is not technique 112.

### Technique 1 ("At the point of sleep") — significant doctrinal contradiction
- **Osho:** This is the **gap between waking and sleep** — a "neutral gear." What is revealed there is the *"real being, your authentic existence."* Osho's image: *"a bottomless dark well… the abyss is bottomless. That is why Buddha has called this nothingness emptiness – shunya."* He prescribes a Sufi pre-exercise: imagine falling into a bottomless dark well.
- **AI:** "Being IS love. Pure being, when touched without the ego's interference, feels like unconditional love." "The vulnerability IS the doorway." "Let sleep come from the heart… The heart opening so fully that the body-mind simply dissolves into it."
- The AI imposes a "**being is love**" doctrine on this sutra. Osho's actual teaching here is closer to "**being is shunya / bottomless emptiness**" — Buddhist/Sufi nothingness. This is one of the more aggressive doctrinal additions in the entire set of files.
- The AI also drops Osho's most concrete instruction: this practice **takes about three months of waiting** before the gap is even glimpsed.
- **Verdict: How to Practice — CONTRADICTS Osho on the core teaching of what is revealed (love vs. shunya).**

### Technique 2 ("Devotion frees")
- **Osho:** *"It is simply one word really, because frees is the consequence of devotion."* He does not give a procedure; he describes devotion as a quality available to heart-oriented (vs. scientifically-oriented) people, and frames it as the alternative path running parallel to the dry-attention techniques.
- **AI:** A 10-step procedural recipe — choose a beloved, devotional space, candle, "begin to pour," "give yourself totally," etc. This is plausible bhakti coaching but it's a generic devotional-yoga procedure. None of Osho's specific framing for *this* sutra is in it.
- **Verdict: How to Practice — extrapolated** (defensible bhakti template, but not Osho's actual instruction for this sutra).

### Technique 3 ("Feel love filling you from all sides")
The sutra is not in Osho. The AI's 10-step practice is essentially a metta-bhavana / loving-kindness / "let love in from all directions" meditation. Plausible practice, **but not Osho's**, because Osho is not commenting on this sutra. **Verdict: SUTRA NOT VERBATIM IN OSHO; the entry is AI-authored throughout.**

### Technique 4 ("As you sing, or listen to music")
Same problem — the sutra is a synthetic paraphrase. The 10-step practice (choose music, dim lights, listen from heart, "let the music play you," dissolve into feeling, post-music silence) is reasonable music-meditation coaching but is not anchored in any specific Osho commentary, because there is no specific Osho commentary on this exact line. **Verdict: SUTRA NOT VERBATIM IN OSHO.**

### Technique 5 ("Wherever your mind is settled, feel this moment as the eternal")
The sutra has been altered. Osho's actual sutra is **"Wherever your mind is *wandering*"** — the **moving** mind is the door. The teaching is: change focus from the cloud (the wandering thought) to the sky (the consciousness behind it). It is the "look at the looker" / "host vs. guest" teaching. Lin-Chi shouting "Who is asking 'who am I'?" is the example.

The AI has substituted an "Eckhart-Tolle-style" Power-of-Now reading: drop past and future, feel "this moment is enough," "eternal sufficiency of now." None of that is in Osho's commentary on this sutra. The "this is technique 112" claim is also unsupported — Osho discusses this sutra in Chapter 31, not as the final one.

- **Verdict: Sutra altered; How to Practice — CONTRADICTS Osho** (different teaching applied to a re-worded sutra). Signs and Pitfalls are downstream of this and inherit the same problem.

### Heart file — Common Pitfalls

## File 5 — Imagination techniques (6 techniques)

Sources: Osho Chapters 21 (file _26_), 25 (file _30_), 47 (file _52_), 50 (file _55_), 69 (file _74_), 79 (file _84_).

### Sutra accuracy
- **t1 ("Imagine the unimaginable — the formless, the limitless, the infinite.")** — **NOT FOUND verbatim.** The closest is Osho's sutra *"Suppose you contemplate something beyond perception"* (Chapter 47). The AI's wording is a reframing.
- **t2 ("Feel 'I am' without any content. Just the feeling of existence without any quality.")** — **NOT FOUND verbatim.** This is a Ramana-Maharshi-flavoured paraphrase, not one of Osho's quoted sutras.
- **t3 ("Look upon a bowl without seeing the sides or the material.")** — **matches Osho** (Chapter 21).
- **t4 ("Abide in some place infinitely spacious, clear of trees, of hills, of habitations…")** — Osho's text says "***endlessly* spacious**," not "infinitely." Minor wording difference; same teaching.
- **t5 ("Sweet-hearted one, meditate on knowing and not-knowing, existing and not-existing. Then leave both aside that you may be.")** — **matches Osho** (Chapter 79).
- **t6 ("Enter the sound of your own name and through it, all names die.")** — **the sutra has been altered.** Osho's actual sutra (Chapter 25) is: *"Enter the sound of your name and, through this sound, all sounds."* The AI changed "**all sounds**" → "**all names die**" — converting it from a sound-based / mantra technique into a "semantic satiation, your name loses meaning" technique.

### Technique 6 ("Enter the sound of your name") — clear distortion
- **Osho's teaching:** Use the sound of your name as a doorway — through this single sound, you enter the source of all sound. It's a sound-meditation / mantra technique.
- **AI's reframing:** Repeat your name until it loses meaning (a real psychological phenomenon called *semantic satiation*). When your name becomes meaningless, all names lose meaning, and you encounter "reality without labels."
- The AI's practice is internally coherent and grounded in real cognitive science — but it is **not what Osho is teaching from this sutra**. Osho is not pointing at semantic satiation; he is pointing at the source of sound.
- **Verdict: Sutra altered; How to Practice — CONTRADICTS Osho** (different practice from a re-worded sutra).

### Technique 3 ("Look upon a bowl") — mostly faithful
- **Osho:** Look at the bowl as a whole, not at the sides or material. After a few moments, awareness arises. He links this to the "see as if for the first time" teaching — fresh perception without the labelling mind.
- **AI:** Steps to look at the bowl whole, not analytically, until awareness arises. **Verdict: How to Practice — faithful.**
- Signs and Pitfalls in this entry are still mostly modern phenomenology, but the practice itself is sound.

### Technique 5 ("knowing and not-knowing, existing and not-existing")
- **Osho:** Hold both poles simultaneously, then drop both. He emphasises this as the Buddhist "neti-neti" / "neither this nor that" approach.
- **AI:** Steps to hold both, then "leave both aside." This is faithful in spirit. **Verdict: faithful, but Signs and Pitfalls are modern coaching extensions.**

### Techniques 1, 2, 4 — partial matches

## Cross-file pattern summary

### 1. Sutra-text alterations
The biggest fidelity issue. Several of the 33 sutras across these five files are **silently altered or AI-invented**:
- centering t6: extra sentence added ("Let the centre of your being be the centre of all your senses").
- death t3, t4: not verbatim in Osho.
- heart t3, t4: not verbatim in Osho.
- heart t5: "wandering" replaced with "settled" — opposite meaning.
- imagination t1, t2: not verbatim in Osho.
- imagination t6: "all sounds" replaced with "all names die" — different teaching.

When a sutra is altered, the entire downstream content becomes commentary on something Osho did not teach. This is the most consequential category of error in the audit.

### 2. "Signs It's Working" is a structural fabrication
Across all five files, this section is largely the AI's invention. Osho does not give phenomenological checklists. Specific items repeat across files — "tears that come without sadness," "warm tingling," "click," "drop feeling," "expansion," "spaciousness" — these are modern meditation-app phenomenology rather than Osho's commentary. **There is no straightforward way to fix this section; it is largely AI-authored content presented as if reflecting the source.**

The few faithful "Signs" items I found:
- breath t5: "eyes lock at third eye" + "thoughts as film on screen" (verbatim Osho).
- breath t7: "lucid dreaming" (verbatim Osho).
- death t1: "I am still here even though the body is gone" (the watcher-on-the-hill insight).
- imagination t3: "the bowl seems to dissolve / fresh perception" (in Osho).

That's the entire list. Roughly 4–5 faithful signs across ~120 sign-bullets in five files.

### 3. "Common Pitfalls" — about 70% AI-extrapolated, with a few real Osho warnings buried in
Genuine Osho warnings I found:
- breath t5: "purity first, because at the third eye imagination becomes potent."
- centering t4: "spinal column not straight → can't see inner thread."

The rest of the pitfalls are reasonable modern coaching ("don't use this to escape unpleasant tasks," "treating forgetting as failure"), but they're not in Osho's text.

A small number of pitfalls actively **contradict** Osho:
- centering t3 pitfall: "blink when you must" (Osho: do not blink, see Bodhidharma story).
- centering t4 pitfall: "feel the spine, don't draw a mental picture" (Osho: visualise it).
- centering t9 pitfall: "the sutra uses 'pin' as a metaphor; do NOT injure yourself" (Osho is literal — explicitly says have a friend pierce your back with a pin).
- breath t6 pitfall: "a few seconds an hour is more than most adults manage; that is enough" (Osho: this technique has to be done continuously).

### 4. "How to Practice" — a recurring sanitisation pattern
The AI repeatedly converts Osho's edge-walking instructions into safe, repeatable, daily-friendly routines:
- breath t4 ("breath stopped of itself"): Osho says cannot be practised; AI makes it a 15-minute lying-down session.
- centering t5 ("close seven openings"): Osho says it must be sudden, push through suffocation; AI says do not hold breath, breathe softly through the gap, 5–15 minutes.
- centering t10 ("deprived of strength"): Osho says become heavy as lead; AI says become light/floating.
- centering t8 ("sit only on buttocks"): Osho describes Buddha-posture weight-balancing; AI describes a navasana / boat-pose abdominal hold.
- death t1 ("focus on fire"): Osho prescribes 3-month cremation watching prerequisite; AI prescribes none.
- heart t1 ("at the point of sleep"): Osho prescribes a 3-month wait-for-the-gap practice plus a Sufi falling-into-bottomless-well preliminary; AI prescribes "feel the heart open as defences drop."

In each case the practical form has been made gentler, more domestic, more daily, and (in several cases) doctrinally re-aimed.

### 5. Doctrinal additions
The clearest doctrinal addition is in heart t1, where the AI inserts the teaching "Being IS love" as the discovery of the technique. Osho's actual reading of this sutra leans Buddhist — what is revealed in the gap is *shunya* / bottomless emptiness, not love. This is a substantive theological re-aiming, not a coaching difference.

A milder version of the same pattern appears throughout: Osho's tantric / shunya / "nothingness" / "abyss" framing is consistently softened toward modern positive-affect language ("warmth," "tenderness," "love," "spaciousness," "freedom").
